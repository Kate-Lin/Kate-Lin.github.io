新年第一篇周记，写了好多噢，要是每次开组会之前就能看一看相关内容就好了。

I mainly learned the following knowledge this week:

  • I read the paper CAKE: Code-Based Algorithm for Key Encapsulation presented at the group meeting this week, and learned the knowledge of encoding-based encryption schemes. I reviewed the main algorithms of McEliece Scheme based on Goppa codes. I haven't learned about the McEliece Scheme based on Quasi-Cyclic Moderate-Density Parity-Check (QC-MDPC) codes before, so I focused on this part in this group meeting. The reason for introducing this scheme is that Goppa codes requires a particularly large public key and high storage cost. QC-MDPC codes is quasi-cyclic codes, so only one row of the check matrix can be saved to represent an entire matrix, which greatly saves costs. This approach led to key sizes that are just a few thousand bits long, and approximated the distinguishing problem to the decoding problem. However, the decoding of QC-QC-MDPC McEliece is probabilistic and may fail. This causes QC-MDPC McEliece suffer the GJS attack. In this attack, the adversary carefully crafts error patterns and observes whether the decoding process fails or not. The adversary can recover the private key by collecting the decoding failure rate of various error patterns. Since the two typical schemes have their own security risks, on the basis of combining the above two schemes, the author proposes the CAKE scheme. The key generation process is significantly faster at the cost of longer public keys, and completely defeats the GJS attack by employing ephemeral keys, which means new keys are generated at each key exchange. The CAKE scheme is IND-CPA secure and the protocol is SK secure. This is the first time I have read a complete paper about Code-Based PQC, and I have gained a lot. I had pre-examined the pre-knowledge before the group meeting, which also enabled me to keep up with the presenter’s ideas at the meeting.

  • I reviewed the Diffie-Hellman key exchange protocol and STS key exchange protocol that I learned in the Cryptography course. They are two important protocols in the key exchange part. The DH protocol cannot resist the man-in-the-middle attack initiated by the active adversary. STS protocol eliminates this risk and uses digital signatures and certificates for identity authentication. I also reviewed the security of RSA and ElGamal schemes and improved methods, especially the addition of ElGamal to hash.

  • This week I also reviewed the group theory part of abstract algebra and sorted out common methods for proving groups, subgroups, and normal subgroups. In the review process, I found that my proof of group isomorphism was very rusty, and I needed to learn it again.
